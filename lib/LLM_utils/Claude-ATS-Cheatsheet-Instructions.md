# ATS Resume Building Reference — Claude Project Instructions

## Purpose & Success Targets

Use this reference to produce ATS-optimized resumes that achieve:

- ATS parsing accuracy: 95%+
- Keyword optimization score: 85%+
- Human readability: High
- Interview conversion rate: 15–20%
- ATS rejection rate: under 15%
- Time to first interview: under 2 weeks

- All nouns: technologies, tools, concepts

- Company culture keywords

- Education and certification requirements
- Industry-specific jargon and acronyms

## Phase 2: Technical Formatting

### File Format

- **1st choice:** .docx (Microsoft Word) — 99% ATS compatibility
- **2nd choice:** .pdf — 85% ATS compatibility
- **Never use:** .txt, .rtf, .pages, .odt, .html

### Document Specifications

- Page size: 8.5" × 11" (Letter)
- Margins: 0.5"–1" on all sides
- Font: Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, Georgia, or Verdana only
- Body text: 10–12pt
- Name: 14–16pt
- Section headers: 12–14pt
- Line spacing: 1.0–1.15 maximum
- Alignment: Left-aligned throughout (never center or justify body text)

### Required Section Headers (use exact terms)

Tier 1 — Required:

- [FULL NAME] as document header
- CONTACT INFORMATION
- PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY
- WORK EXPERIENCE or PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE
- EDUCATION
- SKILLS or TECHNICAL SKILLS

Tier 2 — Optional (include when relevant):

- CERTIFICATIONS
- PROJECTS
- ACHIEVEMENTS
- ADDITIONAL INFORMATION
- PUBLICATIONS
- VOLUNTEER EXPERIENCE

### Forbidden Elements — Instant ATS Failure

Never include:

- Tables, columns, or text boxes
- Content in headers or footers
- Images, logos, photos, or graphics
- Colored text or backgrounds
- Hyperlinks (write URLs as plain text)
- Special characters (★, ♦, ►, etc.)
- Fancy bullets — use • or – only
- Borders, lines, or shapes
- Multiple columns
- Watermarks, drop caps, or custom fonts

## Phase 3: Content Architecture

### Professional Summary Formula (3–4 lines)

```
Line 1: [Job Title] with [X+ years] experience in [Primary Industry/Function]
Line 2: Proven track record of [quantified achievement matching job requirements]
Line 3: Expert in [top 4–5 skills from job description, comma-separated]
Line 4: [Optional: company pedigree, certifications, or unique differentiator]
```

**Tech role example:** "Senior Software Engineer with 8+ years experience in fintech and e-commerce platforms. Proven track record of delivering scalable systems serving 10M+ users with 99.9% uptime. Expert in Python, AWS, microservices architecture, Docker, and agile methodologies. Previously led engineering teams at Amazon and Capital One."

**Marketing role example:** "Digital Marketing Manager with 6+ years experience in B2B SaaS and e-commerce. Proven track record of increasing lead generation by 150% and reducing customer acquisition costs by 35%. Expert in Google Ads, SEO, marketing automation, analytics, and conversion optimization. Google Ads and HubSpot certified."

### Work Experience Bullet Formula

`[Action Verb] + [What You Did] + [How You Did It] + [Quantified Result] + [Timeframe]`

**Example:** "Led cross-functional team of 12 engineers to redesign user authentication system, resulting in 40% reduction in login failures and 25% improvement in user satisfaction scores within 6 months."

### Action Verb Hierarchy

- **Leadership:** Led, Directed, Managed, Orchestrated, Spearheaded, Championed, Supervised, Oversaw
- **Achievement:** Delivered, Achieved, Exceeded, Accomplished, Secured, Attained, Generated, Produced
- **Improvement:** Optimized, Enhanced, Streamlined, Improved, Increased, Reduced, Accelerated, Transformed
- **Technical:** Developed, Implemented, Designed, Built, Created, Engineered, Architected, Deployed
- **Analysis:** Analyzed, Evaluated, Assessed, Researched, Investigated, Identified, Diagnosed
- **Collaboration:** Collaborated, Partnered, Coordinated, Facilitated, Supported, Contributed

### Quantification Requirements

Every bullet point must include at least one metric. Required types:

- Percentages: "Increased efficiency by 35%"
- Dollar amounts: "Generated $2.5M in additional revenue"
- Time savings: "Reduced processing time from 4 hours to 45 minutes"
- Team size: "Led team of 15 cross-functional professionals"
- User numbers: "Improved experience for 100K+ monthly active users"
- Frequency: "Delivered 15+ projects on time and under budget"
- Scope: "Managed $5M annual budget across 3 business units"
- Quality metrics: "Achieved 99.9% uptime and 4.8/5 customer satisfaction"

**Quantification templates:**

- Scale: "Managed/Led/Oversaw [number] of [people/projects/accounts]"
- Growth: "Increased [metric] by [percentage] over [timeframe]"
- Efficiency: "Reduced [process/cost/time] by [amount/percentage]"
- Revenue: "Generated/Saved $[amount] through [specific action]"
- Quality: "Achieved [percentage] [quality metric] rate"
- Timeline: "Delivered [project] on time within [timeframe]"

## Phase 4: Keyword Optimization

### Keyword Extraction Steps

1. Extract every noun from job description: technologies, tools, methodologies, concepts
2. List all required skills and qualifications
3. Identify industry-specific terminology and certifications
4. Capture soft skills and company culture keywords

### Keyword Frequency Targets

- **Primary keywords** (job title variations, core technical skills, required certs): 3–5 occurrences
- **Secondary keywords** (preferred qualifications, industry terms, culture terms): 2–3 occurrences
- **Tertiary keywords** (related technologies, supplementary qualifications): 1–2 occurrences

### Keyword Placement Priority

- **Tier 1 — Critical (100% match):** Professional Summary (first 50 words), Skills section, job titles in Work Experience, first bullet of most recent role
- **Tier 2 — High impact (75% match):** Throughout work experience bullets, Education coursework, Certifications, Project descriptions
- **Tier 3 — Supporting (50% match):** Achievements, Additional Information, Volunteer Experience

### Keyword Density

- Target overall keyword density: 2–4% of total document
- Maximum density per keyword: 5%
- Avoid over-optimization — natural language flow must be preserved

### Acronym Protocol

Always spell out the full term on first use, then use the acronym:
"Search Engine Optimization (SEO)" → "SEO" in subsequent mentions

When space allows, use dual form throughout:
"Managed AWS (Amazon Web Services) infrastructure"

Common pairs to handle this way: API, SDK, CRM, ERP, KPI, ROI, ML, AI, UX, UI

### Contextual Integration

- Integrate keywords within complete sentences
- Use synonyms and natural variations
- Avoid mechanical repetition

Poor: "Python Python developer Python programming"
Good: "Senior Python developer specializing in full-stack development using Python, Django, and Flask frameworks"

## Phase 6: Big Tech Company Customization

### Amazon

Demonstrate Amazon's Leadership Principles through specific examples:

1. Customer Obsession: "Reduced customer support tickets by 40% through proactive user experience improvements"
2. Ownership: "Took full ownership of legacy system migration, ensuring zero downtime"

## Phase 7: ATS Technical Validation

### Pre-Submission File Checklist

- Document saved as .docx
- File name: FirstName_LastName_Resume.docx
- File size under 5MB
- No password protection
- No embedded objects or links
- Single-page orientation (portrait)

### Formatting Validation

- Single-column layout throughout
- Standard fonts only
- Consistent font sizing (10–12pt body, 14–16pt name)
- Left-aligned text
- Standard bullet points (• or –)
- No special characters or symbols
- No colored text, backgrounds, or images

### Content Validation

- Contact information in main body, not in header/footer
- Consistent date formatting (MM/YYYY)
- Standard section headers
- Complete contact information with area code and City, State
- Professional email address
- No typos or grammatical errors

### ATS Parsing Test

1. Copy the entire resume text
2. Paste into a plain text editor
3. Verify all information is readable and properly structured
4. Check that dates, names, and contact info are intact
5. Confirm no information is missing or corrupted
6. Verify no special characters became symbols

### Common Parsing Failures

- Contact info in header/footer — not parsed by most ATS
- Tables breaking content flow
- Special characters rendering as symbols
- Inconsistent date formatting
- Missing section headers
- Corrupted formatting from complex layouts

## Phase 8: Human Readability Optimization

### Visual Hierarchy

- Name: Largest font, top of document
- Section headers: Bold, 12–14pt
- Job titles: Bold, same size as body text
- Company names: Bold or italics
- Dates: Right-aligned or clearly separated
- Bullet points: Consistent formatting throughout

### White Space

- 0.5–1 line spacing between sections
- Single space after bullet points
- Consistent margins
- No orphaned lines at page breaks
- Balanced content distribution

### Bullet Point Rules

- Maximum 2 lines per bullet
- Start with a strong action verb
- Include a quantified result
- Use parallel grammatical structure
- Avoid redundant information

### Section Length Guidelines

- Professional Summary: 3–4 lines
- Work Experience: 3–5 bullets per role
- Skills: 15–25 total skills
- Education: 2–3 lines per degree
- Total document: 1–2 pages maximum

## Advanced Optimization

### Dynamic Keyword Weighting

When prioritizing keyword placement and frequency, apply this weighting:

- Job title keywords: 5×
- Required skills: 4×
- Years of experience: 3×
- Preferred qualifications: 2×
- Company culture terms: 1×

Aim to optimize keyword distribution across sections to maximize the weighted total score.

### Troubleshooting Common Issues

**Keyword stuffing** — Symptom: unnatural language flow → Reduce density, use synonym variations

**Over-formatting** — Symptom: complex layouts breaking ATS parsing → Simplify to single-column format

**Under-quantification** — Symptom: vague achievement statements → Add specific metrics and timeframes

**Generic content** — Symptom: low relevance scores → Customize bullet points for the specific job requirements

**Contact info not extracted** — Solution: move contact details to main document body

**Dates showing as text** — Solution: use MM/YYYY format consistently

**Skills not categorized** — Solution: use standard section headers exactly as listed above

**Job titles not recognized** — Solution: use industry-standard title terminology
